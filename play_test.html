How to Play — Over
あいこ蓄積 / ジャックポット

あいこで積み上がったカードを、次の勝者がすべて奪う。

1
PREPARE — 準備
各プレイヤーは手札10枚を裏向きで持ちます。
2
SELECT — カードを選ぶ
全員、伏せたまま1枚を選び、場の中央に裏向きで出します。
3
REVEAL — 同時公開
「SENO!」の合図で、全員同時にカードを公開します。
4
TAKE OR POT — 獲得 / 持ち越し
単独勝者がいる場合、そのプレイヤーが場の全カードと、POTに蓄積されたカードをすべて獲得します。あいこの場合、出されたカードはPOT（共有プール）に蓄積され、次のラウンドに持ち越されます。
あいこが続くほど、
次に奪えるカードは増えていく。
5
END — 終了
これを、手札がなくなるまで繰り返します。ゲーム終了時、獲得したカードの合計点が最も高いプレイヤーの勝利です。

OVER is the recommended mode for your first game of SENO.

Every player plays one card at the same time. A sole winner takes every card in play.

When the round ends in a tie, the played cards remain in the POT. The next player to win takes everything that has built up.

Win a round and take the cards played that turn. At the end of the game, the player with the highest total score wins.
The more points you play,

How to Play — Over
TIE ACCUMULATION / JACKPOT MODE

When cards build up in a tie, the next winner takes them all.

1
PREPARE
Each player takes a 10-card hand and keeps it face down.
2
SELECT
Without revealing it, each player chooses one card and places it face down in the center.
3
REVEAL
On the call of SENO!, all players reveal their cards at the same time.
4
TAKE OR POT
If there is a sole winner, that player takes every card played in the round, plus every card already stored in the POT. If the round ends in a tie, all played cards are added to the POT and carried over to the next round.
The longer the tie continues,
the more the next win is worth.
5
END OF GAME
Continue until every player has played all cards in their hand. At the end of the game, the player with the highest total value among the cards they have taken wins.
Other Modes

Different kinds of tension.

Points that vanish. A move you cannot choose. Two visible options. A spoken bluff. A battle of memory.

The cards stay the same. The game does not.

VANISHTIE ELIMINATION / COLD MODE
VIEW RULES
Cards played into a tie disappear without ever becoming points.
HOW TO PLAY

Play proceeds in the same way as OVER.

Each player selects one card face down. On the call of “SENO!”, all cards are revealed at the same time.

If there is a sole winner, that player takes only the cards played in that round. There is no POT in VANISH.

If the round ends in a tie, all cards played that round are removed from the game.

Every high-value card
demands a harder decision.
DARKFATE MODE / ZERO STRATEGY
VIEW RULES
You do not choose your move. Your face-down deck chooses for you.
HOW TO PLAY

At the beginning of the game, each player shuffles their 10-card hand and places it face down as a deck.

No player may look at the contents of their deck.

Each round, every player places the top card of their deck directly into play. There is no choice of card.

On the call of “SENO!”, all cards are revealed at the same time.

Matchup resolution is the same as in OVER. If the round ends in a tie, the played cards are added to the POT.

DUALTWO-CHOICE MODE / OPEN INFORMATION
VIEW RULES
Both options are visible. Read which one your opponent will play.
HOW TO PLAY

Each player shuffles their 10-card deck and keeps it face down.

Each player draws the top two cards and places them face up in front of themselves. Every player can see every available option.

On the call of “SENO!”, each player chooses one of their two face-up cards and plays it into the center.

The unused card remains face up in front of that player.

Matchup resolution is the same as in OVER. If the round ends in a tie, the played cards are added to the POT.

In DUAL, LUCK is worth 0 points. It defeats ROCK, SCISSORS and PAPER, but LUCK versus LUCK is a tie.

At the beginning of the next round, each player draws one new card and places it face up beside their remaining card, returning to two available choices.

In the tenth and final round, each player plays their single remaining card with no choice left to make.

If the final round ends in a tie, no player may claim the cards in the POT. The game ends immediately.

The player with the highest total value among the cards they have taken wins.

Visible does not mean predictable.
SHOUTDECLARATION / BLUFF MODE
VIEW RULES
Declare the card you will play. You do not have to tell the truth.
HOW TO PLAY

Each player holds their cards in hand.

While looking at their own hand, players take turns declaring aloud what they intend to play.

A declaration may be true or false. For example: SCISSORS or LUCK.

After every player has made a declaration, each player hides their hand from the others and privately chooses one card to play.

On the call of “SENO!”, all players place their chosen card into play at the same time.

Matchup resolution is the same as in OVER. If the round ends in a tie, the played cards are added to the POT.

The moment rock-paper-scissors becomes poker.
MEMORY SPDMEMORY / SIGN MATCH
VIEW RULES
Match the hand sign, take the cards. Even different values can pair.
HOW TO PLAY

Place all 40 cards face down in the play area.

On their turn, a player selects two cards and turns them face up.

Even if their point values or colors are different, the cards form a pair if they show the same hand sign.

LUCK forms a pair only with another LUCK.

In MEMORY SPD, each LUCK card is worth 8 points. A LUCK pair is worth 16 points.

When a player finds a pair, they take those cards and immediately turn over two more cards.

If the two cards do not form a pair, they are turned face down again and play passes to the next player.

The game ends when no cards remain in the play area.

The player with the highest total value among the cards they have taken wins.

Remember the shape.
Count the points after you take them.
MEMORY STDMEMORY / EXACT MATCH
VIEW RULES
Only cards with the same value and the same hand sign form a pair.
HOW TO PLAY

Place all 40 cards face down in the play area.

On their turn, a player selects two cards and turns them face up.

The cards form a pair only if their point value or color and their hand sign match exactly.

LUCK forms a pair only with another LUCK.

In MEMORY STD, each LUCK card is worth 5 points. A LUCK pair is worth 10 points.

When a player finds a pair, they take those cards and immediately turn over two more cards.

If the two cards do not form a pair, they are turned face down again and play passes to the next player.

The game ends when no cards remain in the play area.

The player with the highest total value among the cards they have taken wins.

Exact memory.
Exact reward.
